RELEVANT PROJECTS

Impact of Aortic Aneurysm Endografts on Systemic Haemodynamics,

Individual project

Course: Computational Haemodynamics, Lecturer: Prof. Lucas Omar Muller

Using a 1D cardiovascular model, the results of 10.1002/cnm.2598 are reproduced. The same blood vessel network is replicated, an endograft and endoleak are modeled. Results are discussed and compared with the scientific literature in a 20-page report that meets the formatting requirements for paper submission.

Modelling the impact of Aortic Valve Stenosis,

Individual project

Course: Biomedical Applications of Mathematics, Lecturer: Prof. Lucas Omar Muller

An aortic valve stenosis is simulated using a closed-loop model, also taking into account the influence of baroreflex. In a 20-minute presentation, the main pressure indexes, as well as flow and pressure curves, are discussed, comparing what was obtained with the literature.

Biophysics of the Cardiovascular System, Mathematical Modeling, and Marfan Syndrome,

Seminar 2hrs

Course: Molecular and Cellular Biophysics, Lecturer: Prof. Marina Scarpa

The seminar provides an overview of mathematical modeling in cardiovascular physiology, introducing principles of physics, fluid dynamics, and fundamental mathematics. The Windkessel model is highlighted as a case study, and the cardiovascular implications of Marfan syndrome are analyzed.

Fighting Tumor: General and Prostate Cancer Models Comparison,

Group project

Course: Mathemal Biology, Lecturer: Prof. Simone Pezzuto

Two models of tumor dynamics, immunotherapy and chemotherapy response, are studied. The results are reproduced and their accuracy and realism are discussed by comparing them with the scientific literature. The conclusions of the study are outlined in a 34-page report and a 48-slide presentation.

Design of a Skin Patch for Nicotine Delivery, Development of a Compartmentalised Model for Inulin Injection, Simulation of Diffusive Motion,

Individual project

Course: Physiological flow and transport in porous tissues, Lecturer: Prof. Alberto Bellin

Using the chemical characteristics of nicotine, a medical patch is designed to function for 24 hours; a 2D diffusive motion is simulated using the Brownian motion; using a two-compartment model, the inulin concentration following an injection is replicated and the results are compared with medical measurements.

Individual project

Course: Numerical methods for PDEs, Lecturer: Prof. Robert N端rnberg

Using FreeFem, an elliptic problem is addressed within a defined domain by applying two boundary conditions.
